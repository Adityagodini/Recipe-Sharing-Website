Panchmel Dal

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/4 cup moong dal (yellow lentils)
  • 1/4 cup toor dal (arhar dal)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal
  • 1/4 cup masoor dal (red lentils)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1-inch ginger (grated)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Dal
- In a pressure cooker, combine all the lentils (moong, toor, chana, masoor, and urad dal)
- with enough water and cook for 3-4 whistles.
- Once the pressure is released, open the lid and check if the dal is soft and fully cooked.
- Mash it slightly to give it a thicker consistency.
Step 2: Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
- Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
- Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
- Add chopped tomatoes, ginger, garlic, green chilies, and cook until the tomatoes soften.
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
- Stir and cook for a minute until the spices release their aroma.
Step 3: Combine Dal and Tadka
- Add the cooked dal to the pan with the tadka (tempering). Stir well to combine.
- Add water to adjust the consistency if needed.
- Let the dal simmer for 5-7 minutes to absorb the flavors of the tadka.
Step 4: Final Touch
- Add garam masala and mix well.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Step 5: Serve
- Serve hot with roti, paratha, or steamed rice. Enjoy your hearty and delicious Panchmel Dal!
