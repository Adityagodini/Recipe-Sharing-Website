Mahcer Jhol

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fish (Rohu, Katla, or any preferred fish, cut into pieces)
  • 2 medium onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 1 tsp panch phoron (Bengali five-spice mix)
  • 1-2 dried bay leaves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1-2 tbsp sugar (optional, for sweetness)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean and wash the fish pieces thoroughly. Pat them dry with a kitchen towel.
- Sprinkle turmeric powder and salt over the fish and set aside for about 10 minutes.
Step 2: Temper the Oil
- Heat mustard oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Once the oil is hot.
- add panch phoron, bay leaves, green cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick.
- Let them splutter and release their aroma.
Step 3: Sauté the Onions and Tomatoes
- Add the sliced onions to the oil and sauté until golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until the tomatoes soften and blend with the onions.
Step 4: Add the Spices
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, and coriander powder.
- Stir the mixture well and cook for a few minutes until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
Step 5: Add Water and Simmer
- Pour in 3-4 cups of water to create a broth. Stir well and bring to a boil.
- Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 5-6 minutes, allowing the spices to infuse the broth.
Step 6: Add the Fish
- Carefully add the marinated fish pieces into the curry.
- Let it simmer for about 10-12 minutes on low heat until the fish is cooked through.
Avoid stirring too much to prevent the fish from breaking.
Step 7: Adjust the Seasoning
- Taste the curry and adjust the salt and sugar (optional) as needed.
Step 8: Garnish and Serve
- Once the fish is cooked, garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve Macher Jhol hot with steamed rice for a delicious Bengali meal!
