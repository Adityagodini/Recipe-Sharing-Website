Fish Gassi

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fish (preferably firm fish like Kingfish or Seer fish)
  • 2 large onions (finely sliced)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut (fresh or desiccated)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste (or 2 tbsp tamarind extract)
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp jaggery (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1-2 green chilies (slit, adjust for spice)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil (coconut oil or vegetable oil)
  • Water as required for curry consistency

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Coconut Paste
- In a blender, combine grated coconut with a little water to make a smooth paste. Set aside.
Step 2: Temper the Spices
- Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
Step 3: Cook the Onions and Tomatoes
- Add the finely sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
- Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
- Add the chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and coriander powder.
- Cook until the tomatoes soften and oil starts to separate from the masala.
Step 4: Add the Coconut Paste and Tamarind
- Add the prepared coconut paste to the pan, along with tamarind paste and some water to adjust the consistency of the gravy.
- Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
Step 5: Cook the Fish
- Gently add the fish pieces into the curry, ensuring that the pieces are submerged in the gravy.
- Cover and cook for about 10-15 minutes, depending on the thickness of the fish.
- Be careful not to stir too much, as the fish may break.
Step 6: Add Coconut Milk and Final Seasoning
- Once the fish is cooked, add coconut milk to the curry for a rich and creamy texture. Stir gently and cook for another 5 minutes.
- Add jaggery for a slight sweetness, adjust salt, and add green chilies if more heat is desired.
Step 7: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve the Fish Gassi hot with steamed rice, neer dosa, or appam for a delicious South Indian meal.
