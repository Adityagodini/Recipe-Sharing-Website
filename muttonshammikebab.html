Mutton Shammi Kebab

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton mince (preferably with some fat)
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, minced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • 1/2 cup chana dal (split chickpeas), soaked for 2 hours
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil (for frying)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Water as required for cooking

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Mutton
- In a large pot, add the mutton mince, soaked chana dal, garlic, ginger, green chilies, cumin seeds,.
- turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, red chili powder, and salt.
- Add enough water to cover the ingredients.
- Cook on medium heat for 30-40 minutes until the meat is tender and the dal is cooked.
- You can use a pressure cooker for faster cooking (approximately 2-3 whistles).
- Once cooked, let the mixture cool down. Then, drain any excess water.
- but reserve a little bit of the cooking liquid for blending.
Step 2: Blend the Mixture
- Transfer the cooked mutton and dal mixture to a food processor or blender.
- Add chopped mint and coriander leaves, along with the lemon juice.
- Blend the mixture into a smooth paste. If needed.
- add a little bit of the reserved cooking liquid to achieve the desired consistency.
- Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
Step 3: Shape the Kebabs
- Take small portions of the mutton paste and shape them into small, round or oval patties using your hands.
- If you prefer, you can shape them into kebab-like cylinders or discs.
- Optionally, you can dip the patties in a lightly beaten egg to help them bind better and give them a crispier texture when frying.
Step 4: Fry the Kebabs
- Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
- Once the oil is hot, gently slide the shaped kebabs into the oil.
- Fry them in batches, making sure they do not overlap.
- Fry the kebabs for 4-5 minutes on each side or until they turn golden brown and crispy.
- Remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
Step 5: Serve
- Serve the hot Mutton Shami Kebabs with a side of green chutney and onion rings.
- You can also serve them with lemon wedges.
Bonus Tip:
- For extra flavor, you can refrigerate the kebabs for 30 minutes before frying to help them firm up and retain their shape better.
