    For Marination:

  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

    • For Frying:

  • 3 tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 onions (sliced thinly)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit lengthwise)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 sprigs curry leaves
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp vinegar (optional for tanginess)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

üë©‚Äçüç≥Recipe

Marinate the Chicken:
- In a bowl, mix chicken with turmeric powder, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, and salt.
- Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably longer for better flavor.
Prepare the Frying Spices:
- Heat coconut oil in a large pan or wok. Add fennel seeds and let them splutter.
- Add sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown and crispy.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies, saut√© for another minute until fragrant.
Add Tomatoes and Spices:
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil begins to separate.
- Add red chili powder, coriander powder, black pepper powder, and salt.
-Stir well and cook the masala for 5-7 minutes until the oil separates from the paste.
Fry the Chicken:
- Add the marinated chicken to the pan, mixing well with the masala.
- Cook on medium heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is well coated and cooked through.
- Add vinegar (optional) to balance the flavors with a tangy kick.
Final Frying:
- Once the chicken is cooked, increase the heat to high and fry the chicken with the masala until.
-the chicken becomes slightly crispy and golden brown.
- Add fresh curry leaves and cook for a few more minutes until the chicken is dry-fried and crispy.
Garnish and Serve:
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
- Best served with Kerala parotta, appam, or steamed rice.
