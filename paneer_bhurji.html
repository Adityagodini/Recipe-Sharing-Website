Panner Bhurji

INGREDIENTS

  • 200g paneer (grated or crumbled)
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 large tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/4 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/4 cup milk (optional, for softer texture)

Recipe

Step 1: Heat Oil and Sauté Spices
- Heat oil or ghee in a pan on medium heat.
- Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Then add the finely chopped green chilies and ginger-garlic paste.
- Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
Step 2: Add Onions and Tomatoes
- Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
- Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and turn into a paste.
Step 3: Add Dry Spices
- Now, add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala powder.
- Mix well and cook for 1-2 minutes.
Step 4: Add Crumbled Paneer
- Add the crumbled or grated paneer to the pan. Mix thoroughly, ensuring the paneer is coated with the masala.
Step 5: Cook the Bhurji
- If the mixture is too dry, add milk for a softer consistency (optional).
- Stir well and cook for 3-4 minutes on low heat, allowing the flavors to meld.
Step 6: Garnish & Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with roti, paratha, or as a side dish with rice.
