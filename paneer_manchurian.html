Panner Manchurian

INGREDIENTS

  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water (for batter)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp oil (for frying)
  • 2 tbsp oil (for sauce preparation)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilies (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp capsicum (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp spring onion (chopped, for garnish)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Batter
- In a bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder,
- white pepper powder, soy sauce, vinegar, and salt.
- Add water gradually to make a smooth batter.
Step 2: Fry the Paneer
- Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
- Dip the paneer cubes in the batter and fry them until golden brown and crispy.
- Remove the paneer cubes and set them aside on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.
Step 3: Prepare the Manchurian Sauce
- In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté the chopped onions and green chilies until they turn translucent.
- Add the chopped capsicum and sauté for another 2 minutes.
- Add soy sauce, vinegar, and a pinch of sugar for sweetness. Stir well to combine.
- Add a little water to make a saucy consistency and bring it to a simmer.
Step 4: Combine and Toss
- Add the fried paneer cubes to the pan with the sauce.
- Toss gently to coat the paneer with the sauce. Cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
Step 5: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with chopped spring onions.
- Serve the Paneer Manchurian hot as a starter or snack with fried rice or noodles.
