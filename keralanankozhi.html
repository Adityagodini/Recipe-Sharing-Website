    For Marination:

  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

    • For Gravy:

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil (for authentic Kerala flavor)
  • 1 large onion (sliced thinly)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit lengthwise)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1 cup coconut milk (fresh or canned)
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

üë©‚Äçüç≥Recipe

Marinate the Chicken:
- In a bowl, mix chicken with turmeric powder, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, and salt.
- Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
Prepare the Gravy:
- Heat coconut oil in a large pan or pressure cooker. Add fennel seeds and curry leaves, let them splutter.
- Add the thinly sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies, cook for a minute until fragrant.
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until the tomatoes become soft and release oil from the masala.
Add the Spices:
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
-Stir well and cook the masala for 5-7 minutes until it releases its aroma.
Cook the Chicken:
- Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pan and cook on medium heat for 10-12 minutes.
-stirring occasionally to coat the chicken with the masala.
- Add water to adjust the gravy consistency and let it cook for another 5 minutes.
Add Coconut Milk:
- Pour in the coconut milk, stir gently, and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes
-until the chicken is tender and the flavors have blended.
Garnish and Serve:
- Sprinkle black pepper powder and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with Kerala-style parotta, appam, or steamed rice.
