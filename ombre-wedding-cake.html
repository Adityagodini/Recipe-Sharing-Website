Ombre Wedding Cake

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • Buttercream frosting (for crumb coat and decoration)
  • Gel food coloring (for ombre effect, use at least 3 shades of your chosen color)
  • Edible pearls or other decorative elements (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Preheat & Prepare
- Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and line your cake pans with parchment paper.
Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients
- In a bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
Step 3: Cream Butter & Sugar
- Beat together butter and sugar until the mixture becomes light and fluffy.
- Add eggs one at a time, followed by vanilla extract.
Step 4: Add Dry Ingredients & Milk
- Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the milk.
- Mix until smooth and combined.
Step 5: Divide the Batter
- Once the batter is ready, divide it into three (or more) bowls.
- depending on how many layers of color you want for the ombre effect.
- The number of bowls should correspond with the number of shades you plan to create.
Step 6: Add Food Coloring
- To each bowl, add a few drops of gel food coloring.
- Start with the lightest shade for the first bowl, then gradually add more coloring to the remaining bowls for darker shades.
- Mix until the color is evenly incorporated.
Step 7: Bake the Cakes
- Pour the colored batter into separate cake pans, ensuring each layer is even.
- Bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
- Allow the cakes to cool completely.
Step 8: Apply Crumb Coat
- Once the cakes are cooled, apply a thin layer of buttercream frosting to seal in the crumbs.
- Chill the cake in the fridge for 30 minutes to set the crumb coat.
Step 9: Frost the Cake
- After the crumb coat is set, apply a thicker layer of buttercream frosting to each tier of the cake.
- Start with the darkest color on the bottom tier, working your way up to the lightest color on the top tier.
- Smooth the frosting to create a gradient effect that transitions from dark to light as you move upward.
Step 10: Blend the Colors
- To create a smooth ombre effect.
- use a bench scraper or offset spatula to gently blend the colors from one tier to the next.
- You can also add a bit of extra buttercream between layers if needed to smooth out the transitions.
Step 11: Decorate the Cake
- Once the ombre effect is achieved, decorate the cake with edible pearls, flowers.
- any other decorative elements that complement the wedding theme.
Step 12: Assemble & Serve
- Carefully stack the tiers of cake on a sturdy cake stand.
- making sure each tier is level and supported with cake dowels if necessary.
- Once assembled, slice and enjoy the beautiful ombre wedding cake!
