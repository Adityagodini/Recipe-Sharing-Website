Prawn Cutlet

INGREDIENTS

  • 300g prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 1 medium potato, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten (for binding)
  • Breadcrumbs (for coating)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 tbsp rice flour (optional, for crispiness)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Prawns
- Wash the prawns thoroughly and pat them dry with a kitchen towel.
- Finely chop the prawns into small pieces. Alternatively, you can pulse them in a food processor for a finer texture.
Step 2: Cook the Prawns
- Heat a little oil in a pan and add the finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
- Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
- Add the chopped prawns, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
- Cook for about 5 minutes or until the prawns are fully cooked.
Step 3: Prepare the Filling
- In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked prawns with the mashed potatoes, lemon juice, and fresh coriander.
- Mix thoroughly to ensure that all ingredients are well combined. Adjust the seasoning if necessary.
Step 4: Form the Cutlets
- Take small portions of the mixture and shape them into round or oval cutlets.
- If the mixture is too soft to shape, add a little rice flour or breadcrumbs to bind it together.
Step 5: Coat the Cutlets
- Dip each cutlet in the beaten egg, ensuring it is fully coated.
- Roll the egg-coated cutlet in breadcrumbs to coat it evenly.
- You can also double coat it by dipping it in egg and breadcrumbs again for extra crunch.
Step 6: Fry the Cutlets
- Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
- Once the oil is hot, gently slide in the cutlets and fry them until they are golden brown and crispy on both sides.
Step 7: Drain and Serve
- Remove the cutlets from the oil and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
- Serve hot with green chutney or tamarind chutney and enjoy!
