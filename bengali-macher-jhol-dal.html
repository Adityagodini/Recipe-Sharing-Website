Bengali Macher Jhol Dal

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup moong dal (yellow split gram)
  • 2 medium fish pieces (preferably Rohu or Katla)
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Water as needed

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Dal
- Wash the moong dal thoroughly and pressure cook it with enough water until soft and mushy.
- Once cooked, set it aside to keep warm.
Step 2: Fry the Fish
- Heat 1 tablespoon of mustard oil in a pan.
- Add the fish pieces and fry them lightly until golden on both sides. Set aside.
Step 3: Prepare the Curry Base
- In the same pan, add the remaining mustard oil.
- Add mustard seeds and dried red chilies. Let them splutter.
- Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
- Add ginger paste and cook for another minute.
- Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and red chili powder.
- Stir well and cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil separates from the masala.
Step 4: Prepare the Jhol
- Add water to the pan to make a thin gravy, adjusting the quantity to your desired consistency.
- Add sugar, salt, and the garam masala powder. Stir and bring the gravy to a boil.
- Once the gravy is boiling, add the fried fish pieces back into the pan. Simmer for about 5-6 minutes.
Step 5: Combine Dal and Fish Curry
- Gently mix the cooked moong dal with the fish curry, ensuring the fish pieces are intact.
- Simmer for another 5 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
- Serve with steamed rice for an authentic Bengali meal.
