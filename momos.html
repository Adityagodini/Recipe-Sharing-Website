Momos

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp oil (for dough)
  • 1/4 cup water (adjust as needed for dough)
  • 1 tbsp oil (for greasing)
  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1/2 cup boiled and finely chopped chicken or paneer (optional)
  • 1-2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • Salt to taste

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Dough
- In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt.
- Add 1 tbsp oil and mix it into the flour.
- Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead the dough until it is smooth and firm.
- Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
Step 2: Prepare the Filling
- In a pan, heat 1 tbsp oil and saut√© the garlic, onions, and vegetables until they soften.
- Add the chopped chicken or paneer if using, and stir-fry for a few minutes.
- Add soy sauce, sesame oil, black pepper powder, and salt. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
- Remove from heat and let the mixture cool down.
Step 3: Make the Momos
- Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a thin circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter).
- Place a spoonful of the filling mixture in the center of each circle.
- Fold the edges of the dough to form a pleated pouch, pinching them together at the top to seal the momo.
Step 4: Steam the Momos
- Grease a steamer tray with oil and place the momos in the tray, leaving some space between each one.
- Steam the momos in a preheated steamer for about 15-20 minutes or until the dough is cooked and becomes translucent.
Step 5: Serve
- Remove the momos from the steamer and serve hot with a dipping sauce or chutney of your choice.
Bonus Tips:
- You can use a combination of vegetables like mushrooms, spinach, or corn in the filling for extra flavor.
- For a crispy version, pan-fry the steamed momos until golden brown.
- If you prefer a vegetarian version, skip the chicken or paneer and use more vegetables like cabbage, carrots, and mushrooms.
