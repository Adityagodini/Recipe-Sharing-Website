Goan Recheado Masala Fry

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fish (preferably Kingfish, Pomfret, or Mackerel)
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder (adjust to spice level)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 2 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 2 tbsp vinegar (preferably Goan vinegar)
  • 1/4 cup fresh grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 tbsp oil (for frying)
  • Salt to taste

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Masala Paste
- In a blender or mortar and pestle, combine red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder.
- coriander powder, ginger-garlic paste, tamarind pulp, vinegar, grated coconut, and sugar.
- Grind all the ingredients into a smooth, thick paste. If needed, add a small amount of water to adjust the consistency.
Step 2: Marinate the Fish
- Clean the fish thoroughly and make shallow slashes on both sides of each piece.
- Apply the prepared recheado masala paste generously to the fish pieces, making sure to coat them well, including the slashes.
- Let the fish marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour, allowing the flavors to infuse.
Step 3: Fry the Fish
- Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
- Once the oil is hot, add mustard seeds and let them splutter for a few seconds.
- Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
- Carefully place the marinated fish into the pan and fry on both sides for about 4-5 minutes.
- or until the fish is cooked through and the masala is nicely fried.
- Be gentle while turning the fish to avoid breaking it.
Step 4: Serve
- Once fried, remove the fish from the pan and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve the Goan Recheado Masala Fry hot with steamed rice, chapatis, or a Goan-style salad.
