Drip Wedding Cake

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • Buttercream frosting (for crumb coat and decoration)
  • 200g dark chocolate or white chocolate (for the drip effect)
  • Heavy cream (for the chocolate drip)
  • Edible flowers or other decorations (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Preheat & Prepare
- Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and line your cake pans with parchment paper.
Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients
- In a bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
Step 3: Cream Butter & Sugar
- Beat together butter and sugar until the mixture becomes light and fluffy.
- Add eggs one at a time, followed by vanilla extract.
Step 4: Add Dry Ingredients & Milk
- Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the milk. Mix until smooth and combined.
Step 5: Bake the Cake
- Divide the batter evenly among your prepared cake pans and bake for 25-30 minutes.
- until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cakes to cool completely.
Step 6: Apply Crumb Coat
- Once the cakes have cooled, apply a thin layer of buttercream frosting to seal in the crumbs.
- Chill the cake in the fridge for 30 minutes to set the crumb coat.
Step 7: Prepare the Chocolate Drip
- In a heatproof bowl, melt the chocolate with a little heavy cream over a double boiler or microwave in short intervals.
- Stir until smooth. The drip consistency should be thick enough to stay on the cake but run slightly when applied.
Step 8: Frost the Cake
- After the crumb coat has set, cover the cake with a smooth layer of buttercream frosting.
- Make sure to use a generous amount to create a perfect base for the drip effect.
Step 9: Apply the Chocolate Drip
- Spoon the melted chocolate into a piping bag or a squeeze bottle.
- Gently squeeze the chocolate drip around the edges of the cake, letting it drip down naturally.
- Add drips at the top edge and allow them to set.
Step 10: Decorate the Cake
- Once the drip effect has set, decorate the cake with flowers, sprinkles, or any other decorations you desire.
- You can also pipe additional buttercream rosettes or other designs to enhance the cake.
Step 11: Assemble & Serve
- Carefully transfer the cake to a cake stand and ensure it is centered.
- Slice and serve this stunning Drip Wedding Cake to your guests!
