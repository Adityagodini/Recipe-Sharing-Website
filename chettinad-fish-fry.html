Chettinad Fish Fry

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fish (Kingfish or any firm white fish, cut into steaks)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp black pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp rice flour (for crispy coating)
  • 2 tbsp corn flour
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh curry leaves
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or coconut oil for frying)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean and wash the fish pieces thoroughly. Pat dry with a kitchen towel. Set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Marinade
- In a bowl, mix ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder.
- fennel seeds, cumin powder, black pepper powder, garam masala, lemon juice, tamarind paste, yogurt, and salt.
- Stir well to form a smooth marinade.
Step 3: Marinate the Fish
- Coat the fish pieces with the marinade, ensuring all sides are covered. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
Step 4: Prepare the Coating
- In a separate plate, mix rice flour and corn flour. This will be used to coat the marinated fish to make it crispy.
Step 5: Coat the Fish
- Take each marinated fish piece and coat it evenly with the rice flour-corn flour mixture. Shake off the excess flour.
Step 6: Fry the Fish
- Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the fish pieces and fry them in batches.
- ensuring not to overcrowd the pan.
- Fry each side for about 4-5 minutes or until golden brown and crispy.
- Remove the fried fish from the pan and drain excess oil on paper towels.
Step 7: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish the Chettinad Fish Fry with fresh curry leaves.
- Serve hot with onion rings, lemon wedges, and a side of rice or as a snack.
