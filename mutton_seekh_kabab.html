Mutton Seekh Kabab

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton mince (preferably with some fat)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon powder
  • 1 tbsp chopped mint leaves
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp breadcrumbs (or more as needed)
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil (for grilling)
  • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Kebab Mixture
- In a large bowl, combine mutton mince, finely chopped onions, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste.
- garam masala powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cinnamon powder.
- mint leaves, coriander leaves, lemon juice, salt, black pepper powder, and kasuri methi.
- Mix everything well. You can use your hands to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.
- If the mixture is too loose, add breadcrumbs gradually until it firms up.
- You can also add an egg to help bind the mixture together.
Step 2: Shape the Kebabs
- Divide the mixture into small portions and shape them into long, cylindrical kebabs using your hands.
- The traditional shape is that of a seekh (skewer). If you prefer, you can use skewers to make the kebabs.
- Press gently to ensure they hold their shape and are evenly formed.
Step 3: Grill the Kebabs
- Heat ghee or oil in a grill pan, tawa, or barbecue grill over medium heat.
- Once the pan is hot, place the kebabs on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes on each side until.
- they are browned and cooked through. If you're using a barbecue, grill the kebabs until.
- they're slightly charred on the outside and cooked through on the inside.
- Optionally, brush the kebabs with ghee or oil while grilling to keep them moist and flavorful.
Step 4: Serve
- Once cooked, remove the kebabs from the grill and serve them hot.
- Garnish with chopped onions, lemon wedges, and coriander leaves.
- You can also serve them with green chutney or a yogurt dip.
Bonus Tip:
- For extra flavor, marinate the mutton mixture for a few hours in the fridge before shaping the kebabs.
