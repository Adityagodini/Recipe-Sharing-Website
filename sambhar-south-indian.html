Sambhar South Indian

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1 carrot (chopped)
  • 1 small potato (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup drumsticks (cut into pieces)
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon sambhar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 dry red chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • For Tempering:
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1-2 dry red chilies
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Toor Dal
- Wash the toor dal thoroughly and cook it in a pressure cooker with 3 cups of water.
- and turmeric powder for 3-4 whistles, until soft.
- Once cooked, mash the dal and set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables
- In a separate pot, add chopped carrots, potatoes, drumsticks, and onions.
- Add enough water to cover the vegetables and cook them until they are tender.
- Once cooked, add the chopped tomatoes and cook for 5 more minutes until the tomatoes soften.
Step 3: Prepare the Sambhar Base
- Add the tamarind pulp to the cooked vegetables and mix well.
- Add sambhar powder, salt, and adjust the consistency with water if needed.
- Let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine and the vegetables to absorb the spices.
Step 4: Tempering (Tadka)
- In a small pan, heat oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, dry red chilies, and asafoetida.
- Once the mustard seeds splutter, pour the tempering over the simmering sambhar.
- Stir well and allow it to simmer for another 5 minutes.
Step 5: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with idli, dosa, vada, or rice. Enjoy your delicious South Indian sambhar!
