Sarson Da Saag

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups mustard greens (sarson), washed and chopped
  • 1 cup spinach leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup bathua (chenopodium leaves), optional
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 tablespoon maize flour (makki ka atta)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or clarified butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Greens
- Wash and chop the mustard greens, spinach, and bathua leaves (if using).
- In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
- Add the greens to the boiling water and cook for about 15-20 minutes until tender.
- Drain the cooked greens and blend them into a coarse paste using a blender or a hand blender. Set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Tempering
- Heat ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
- Add finely chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chili, and cook for another 2 minutes.
Step 3: Cook the Tomatoes
- Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
- Cook the mixture until the tomatoes soften and the oil begins to separate from the masala.
Step 4: Add the Greens and Simmer
- Add the blended greens paste to the pan and stir well to combine with the tempering.
- Add water as required to adjust the consistency of the saag.
- Cover and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
Step 5: Add Maize Flour
- In a separate small bowl, mix the maize flour (makki ka atta) with a little water to make a smooth paste.
- Add this paste to the saag, stirring well to thicken the curry.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Add garam masala powder and stir well. Cook for another 5 minutes.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with makki di roti (cornmeal flatbread), and enjoy the authentic taste of Punjab!
