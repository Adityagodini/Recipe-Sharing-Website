Mutton Stew With Appam

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 cup coconut milk (fresh or canned)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Mutton
- Heat coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom pods.
- Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
- Add sliced onions, minced ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Cook until the onions become soft and golden brown.
Step 2: Cook the Mutton
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and blend into the gravy.
- Add the mutton pieces and sauté for 5-6 minutes, ensuring the meat is browned on all sides.
- Sprinkle turmeric powder, black pepper, and salt. Stir to coat the mutton evenly.
Step 3: Add Liquids
- Add water to the pot, bring it to a boil, and then reduce the heat to low.
- Cover and let it simmer for 45-60 minutes, or until the mutton is tender.
Step 4: Add Coconut Milk
- Once the mutton is tender, add coconut milk to the pot.
- Stir well to combine and let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes until the stew thickens slightly.
Step 5: Final Touches
- Add garam masala powder and lemon juice. Stir well and simmer for 2 minutes to allow the flavors to blend together.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves for added flavor and color.
- Serve the mutton stew hot with Appam on the side.
Bonus Tip:
- Appam can be made by fermenting a batter of rice and coconut milk.
- Serve it fresh and warm to complement the stew.
