Black Forest Cake

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup buttermilk, room temperature
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 3 tbsp powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (for whipped cream)
  • 1 jar (14 oz) of Morello cherries or regular cherries, drained
  • 1 tbsp cherry juice (optional, for soaking the cake)
  • Chocolate shavings or grated chocolate, for decoration
  • Fresh cherries, for topping


Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Cake
- Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans or line with parchment paper.
- In a medium bowl, sift together flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
- In a large bowl, whisk together sugar, eggs, oil, buttermilk, and vanilla extract until smooth and combined.
- Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
- Slowly add the boiling water to the batter. The batter will be thin, but that’s perfectly normal.
- Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes.
- or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
- Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
Step 2: Prepare the Whipped Cream
- In a chilled mixing bowl, beat the heavy whipping cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form.
- Continue beating until stiff peaks form. Be careful not to overwhip. Set aside.
Step 3: Assemble the Cake
- Once the cakes have cooled completely, slice each cake in half horizontally to create 4 layers of cake.
- Place the first cake layer on a serving platter. Drizzle with a little cherry juice (or syrup) to soak the cake.
- Spread a generous amount of whipped cream over the cake layer, followed by a layer of cherries.
- Place the second cake layer on top, repeating the process: soak, whip cream, and cherries.
- Continue layering and finishing with a top layer of cake. Cover the entire cake with the remaining whipped cream.
Step 4: Decorate the Cake
- Garnish the top of the cake with chocolate shavings or grated chocolate, ensuring a nice even coating.
- Decorate with fresh cherries and more whipped cream swirls for a classic Black Forest look.
Step 5: Chill and Serve
- Refrigerate the cake for at least 1 hour before serving to allow the layers to set.
- Slice the cake and serve chilled. Enjoy the rich and decadent flavors of Black Forest Cake!
