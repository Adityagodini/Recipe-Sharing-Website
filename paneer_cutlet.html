Panner Cutlet

INGREDIENTS

  • 200g paneer (grated)
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves (finely chopped)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp chat masala (optional)
  • Oil (for shallow frying)
  • Breadcrumbs (for coating)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Mixture
- In a mixing bowl, combine grated paneer and mashed potatoes.
- Add chopped green chilies, coriander leaves, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, cumin powder,
- red chili powder, turmeric powder, chat masala (optional), and salt to the mixture.
- Add lemon juice and mix everything thoroughly. The mixture should hold together well.
- If it's too soft, add breadcrumbs to bind it.
Step 2: Shape the Cutlets
- Take small portions of the mixture and shape them into round or oval cutlets using your hands.
- Roll each cutlet in breadcrumbs to coat it evenly on all sides.
Step 3: Shallow Fry the Cutlets
- Heat oil in a shallow frying pan over medium heat.
- Fry the cutlets until they turn golden brown and crisp on both sides (about 3-4 minutes on each side).
- Do not overcrowd the pan; fry in batches if needed.
Step 4: Drain and Serve
- Once the cutlets are crispy, remove them from the pan and drain excess oil on paper towels.
- Serve hot with green chutney or tomato ketchup for a delightful snack or appetizer.
