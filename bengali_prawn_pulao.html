Bengali Prawn Pulao

INGREDIENTS

  • 250g prawns (cleaned and deveined)
  • 1 1/2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 large onion (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit lengthwise)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala powder
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 1/2 cups water

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Rice
- Rinse the basmati rice well and soak it in water for 15-20 minutes. Drain and set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Prawns
- Marinate the prawns with yogurt, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt for about 15-20 minutes.
Step 3: Fry the Onions
- Heat oil and ghee in a pan, and fry the sliced onions until golden brown and crispy.
- Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnishing.
Step 4: Temper the Spices
- In the same pan, add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon stick.
- Let the spices splutter for a few seconds.
Step 5: Cook the Prawns
- Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Stir for a minute, then add the marinated prawns.
- Cook for 3-4 minutes until the prawns are half-cooked.
Step 6: Add Tomatoes and Rice
- Add chopped tomatoes to the prawns and cook until they turn soft.
- Then add the soaked and drained rice to the pan and stir gently to mix the spices.
Step 7: Cook the Pulao
- Add 2 1/2 cups of water and salt to taste. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
- Cover the pan and cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.
Step 8: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish the Bengali Prawn Pulao with the fried onions and fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with raita or a side salad.
