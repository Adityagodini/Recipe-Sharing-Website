Goan Mutton Xacuti

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in pieces)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh coconut, grated
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds (khus-khus)
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1/4 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste (or lemon juice)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Spices
- In a dry pan, roast the poppy seeds, sesame seeds, black peppercorns, mustard seeds, cinnamon, cloves.
- and cardamom until they release their aroma, about 2-3 minutes. Let them cool slightly.
- Once cooled, grind these roasted spices along with the grated coconut into a smooth paste using a little water. Set aside.
Step 2: Cook the Mutton
- Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
- Add the chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
- Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
- Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil separates from the masala.
Step 3: Add the Ground Paste
- Add the ground coconut and spice paste to the pot, stirring to combine with the onions and tomatoes.
- Cook for 4-5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the paste to cook out.
Step 4: Add the Mutton and Simmer
- Add the mutton pieces to the pot and stir well to coat the meat in the spice mixture.
- Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, red chili powder, and salt. Stir again to combine.
- Add water to the pot and bring it to a boil.
- Reduce the heat, cover, and let the mutton simmer for 1.5-2 hours until the meat is tender.
- Stir occasionally and add more water if needed.
Step 5: Add Tamarind or Lemon Juice
- Once the mutton is tender and the curry has thickened, add the tamarind paste or lemon juice. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
Step 6: Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve the Goan Mutton Xacuti hot with steamed rice or Indian breads like naan or chapati.
Bonus Tip:
- For an extra punch, you can increase the number of green chilies or add some dried red chilies while roasting the spices.
