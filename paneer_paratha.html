Panner Paratha

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 200g paneer (grated)
  • 1/4 cup onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (for dough)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)
  • 1 tbsp ginger (grated, optional)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Filling
- In a bowl, combine grated paneer, chopped onion, green chili, coriander leaves, ginger, and lemon juice.
- Add garam masala, cumin powder, red chili powder, amchur, and salt. Mix everything well to combine.
- Set the filling aside to be used later.
Step 2: Prepare the Dough
- In a large mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt.
- Gradually add water and knead it into a smooth and soft dough.
- Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
Step 3: Stuff and Roll the Paratha
- Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball).
- Roll out each dough ball into a small circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter).
- Place a spoonful of the paneer filling in the center of the dough circle.
- Carefully fold the edges to seal the filling inside,
- then gently roll out the stuffed dough ball into a larger circle (about 6-8 inches in diameter).
Step 4: Cook the Paratha
- Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat and place the rolled-out paratha on it.
- Cook for about 1-2 minutes until bubbles start to form on the surface, then flip it over.
- Apply a little oil or ghee on the flipped side and cook until golden brown spots appear on both sides.
- Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls and filling.
Step 5: Serve
- Serve the hot Paneer Parathas with yogurt, pickle, or any chutney of your choice.
