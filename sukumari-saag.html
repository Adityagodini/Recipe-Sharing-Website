Sukumari Saag

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups fresh sukumari saag (tender leafy greens like spinach or amaranth), finely chopped
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced (optional)
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil (or any preferred cooking oil)
  • 1/2 tsp panch phoron (Bengali five spice mix)
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 tsp sugar (optional, for a touch of sweetness)






Recipe

Step 1: Prep the Greens
- Wash the sukumari saag thoroughly, drain, and finely chop. If using stems, chop them smaller for even cooking.
Step 2: Tempering
- Heat mustard oil in a pan until it begins to smoke. Reduce heat and add panch phoron and red chilies. Let them sizzle.
Step 3: Add Veggies
- Add diced potatoes and sauté with a pinch of salt and turmeric. Cook covered until they’re halfway done.
Step 4: Add the Greens
- Add the chopped saag to the pan. Stir well. Cover and cook on low heat. The greens will release moisture and wilt.
Step 5: Simmer
- Add a pinch of sugar if desired. Let everything simmer together until the potatoes and saag are soft and flavors are blended.
Step 6: Serve
- Serve warm with steamed rice or with hot phulkas. A light and healthy Bengali-style comfort curry!
