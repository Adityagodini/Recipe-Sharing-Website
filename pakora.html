Pakora

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1 medium onion, finely sliced
  • 1 medium potato, grated
  • 1/2 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Batter
- In a large bowl, combine besan, rice flour, cumin seeds, ajwain, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt.
- Add the sliced onions, grated potato, chopped spinach, and chopped green chilies to the flour mixture.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and mix everything well. The moisture from the veggies should be enough to form a thick batter.
- If the mixture feels too dry, add a little water to bring it together into a thick, sticky batter.
Step 2: Heat Oil
- Heat oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium heat.
- To check if the oil is ready, drop a small amount of batter into the oil.
- If it sizzles and rises to the surface immediately, the oil is hot enough.
Step 3: Fry the Pakoras
- Once the oil is hot, drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil.
- carefully shaping them into small fritters with your hands or a spoon.
- Fry the pakoras in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
- Fry until they are golden brown and crispy on all sides. This should take about 4-5 minutes per batch.
Step 4: Drain and Serve
- Remove the pakoras from the oil and drain them on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
- Serve hot with tamarind chutney or mint chutney for dipping.
Bonus Tips:
- You can also make vegetable pakoras by adding other vegetables like cauliflower, zucchini, or carrots.
- For a variation, you can stuff the pakoras with cheese or paneer for a cheesy filling.
