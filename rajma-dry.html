Rajma Dry

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup Rajma (Kidney Beans)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water for cooking

Recipe

Step 1: Soak and Cook the Rajma
- Wash the rajma thoroughly and soak it overnight or for at least 6-8 hours. Drain the water.
- In a pressure cooker, add soaked rajma with 4 cups of water and cook for 3-4 whistles or until they are soft. Set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Tempering
- Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
Step 3: Add Onions, Garlic, and Ginger
- Add finely chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, and garlic. SautÃ© until the onions turn golden brown.
Step 4: Add Tomatoes and Spices
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Stir well and cook for a few minutes.
Step 5: Combine Rajma with Masala
- Add the cooked rajma to the pan. Mix well to coat the rajma in the masala.
- Add a little water if necessary to prevent it from sticking.
Step 6: Cook the Rajma Dry
- Let the rajma cook in the masala on low heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
Step 7: Garnish and Serve
- Once the rajma is well coated and dry, garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
