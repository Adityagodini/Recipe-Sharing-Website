Mutton Rara

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in and boneless pieces)
  • 200g minced mutton (keema)
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • Water as needed

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Mutton and Mince
- Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil in a pot.
- Add the bone-in mutton pieces and brown them on all sides.
- Remove the mutton and set it aside.
- In the same pot, add the minced mutton (keema) and cook until browned and crumbly. Set aside.
Step 2: Sauté Onions and Spices
- In the same pot, add another tablespoon of ghee or oil.
- Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Ground Spices
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil separates.
- Add red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, and fennel seeds.
- Stir well and cook for 3-4 minutes until the spices release their aroma.
Step 4: Combine Mutton and Keema
- Add the browned mutton pieces and cooked keema back into the pot.
- Mix everything well and cook for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine.
Step 5: Add Yogurt and Simmer
- Add yogurt to the mixture and mix until the yogurt blends well with the spices.
- Add water as needed to achieve the desired consistency for the gravy.
- Cover and cook on low heat for 40-45 minutes or until the mutton becomes tender.
- If using a pressure cooker, cook for 4-5 whistles on medium heat.
Step 6: Add Kasuri Methi and Final Seasoning
- Once the mutton is cooked, sprinkle dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) on top.
- Adjust the seasoning by adding salt or chili powder, if needed.
Step 7: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with naan, paratha, or steamed rice.
Bonus Tip:
- For a richer flavor, let the Mutton Rara sit for a few hours before serving, as the flavors will deepen with time.
