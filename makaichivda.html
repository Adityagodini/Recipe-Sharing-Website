Makaichivda

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups cornflakes (preferably thick variety)
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts
  • 1/4 cup roasted chana (gram)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt to taste

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Cornflakes
- In a large mixing bowl, take the cornflakes and lightly crush them with your hands to break them into smaller pieces.
- Set them aside for later use.
Step 2: Fry the Nuts and Seeds
- Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a pan on medium heat.
- Add the peanuts and cashew nuts to the pan and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy.
- Remove them from the pan and set them aside.
- In the same pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and asafoetida (hing). Let them splutter.
Step 3: Add Spices and Green Chilies
- Add chopped green chilies and curry leaves to the pan. Fry them for a minute.
- Sprinkle turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Stir well to coat everything in the spices.
Step 4: Combine Everything
- Add the roasted chana, fried peanuts, and cashew nuts into the pan. Stir to combine the mixture.
- Then add the crushed cornflakes, mix gently to avoid breaking them further.
- and ensure all ingredients are evenly coated with the spices.
Step 5: Final Touches
- Add sugar and salt to taste. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes on low heat.
- Remove from heat once everything is well mixed and the flavors are absorbed.
Step 6: Cool and Serve
- Let the Makai Chivda cool completely before storing it in an airtight container.
- Serve as a crunchy snack with tea or enjoy it anytime as a light snack.
Bonus Tips:
- You can add roasted almonds, dried coconut slices, or raisins for extra flavor.
- Adjust the amount of chili powder and green chilies based on your spice preference.
