Bengalgram Dal Curry

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup Bengal Gram Dal (Chana Dal)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp hing (asafoetida)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water for cooking

Recipe

Step 1: Wash and Soak the Bengal Gram Dal
- Wash the Bengal gram dal thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes to 1 hour.
Step 2: Pressure Cook the Dal
- In a pressure cooker, add the soaked dal with 3-4 cups of water.
- Cook for 3-4 whistles or until the dal becomes soft and tender. Set aside.
Step 3: Prepare the Tempering
- Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and hing. Let them splutter and release their aroma.
Step 4: Add Onions, Garlic, and Ginger
- Add finely chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, and garlic.
- SautÃ© until the onions turn golden brown and aromatic.
Step 5: Add Tomatoes and Spices
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder.
- Stir well and cook for a few minutes until the masala blends together.
Step 6: Combine Cooked Dal with Masala
- Add the cooked Bengal gram dal to the pan. Stir well to combine with the spices and masala.
- Add salt and water as needed to adjust the consistency.
Step 7: Simmer and Finish
- Let the dal simmer on low heat for about 10-15 minutes. Stir occasionally and adjust the consistency if needed.
