Dum Pukht Dal

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup toor dal (yellow pigeon peas)
  • 1/4 cup moong dal (split yellow lentils)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 1 medium onion (finely sliced)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (for smoky flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cream (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Lentils
- Rinse the toor dal, moong dal, and urad dal thoroughly.
- In a pressure cooker, add the soaked dals, turmeric powder, and enough water.
- Cook for 3-4 whistles or until the dals are soft and cooked through.
- Mash the dal lightly, but leave it a bit chunky for texture.
Step 2: Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
- Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds and allow them to splutter.
- Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
- Add the ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
- Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
Step 3: Add Spices
- Add coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, cumin powder, and salt.
- Stir the mixture and cook for a few minutes.
Step 4: Combine Dal and Tadka
- Add the cooked dal to the pan and mix well. If the dal is too thick, add water to adjust the consistency.
- Add the kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) and smoked paprika for a smoky aroma.
- Let the dal simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop and combine.
Step 5: Dum (Slow Cooking)
- Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid, sealing the edges with dough if needed.
- Let it cook on very low heat (dum) for 10-15 minutes.
- This step allows the flavors to infuse and the dal to develop a deep, rich taste.
Step 6: Final Touch
- Stir the dal once, and add fresh cream for richness (optional).
- Adjust seasoning if necessary, adding a dash of lemon juice to enhance the flavors (optional).
Step 7: Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
- Enjoy the aromatic, flavorful Dum Pukht Dal!
