INGREDIENTS

  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 tbsp rice flour
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp curd (yogurt)
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander (for garnish)
  • 2 tbsp oil (for frying)
  • 1-2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/4 tsp ajinomoto (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Marinate the Paneer
- In a bowl, combine ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala,
- rice flour, cornflour, curd, salt, lemon juice, and ajinomoto.
- Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and coat them well. Let it marinate for 30 minutes to an hour.
Step 2: Fry the Paneer
- Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
- Once hot, add the marinated paneer cubes and fry them until they are golden and crispy.
- Remove the paneer and set them aside.
Step 3: Prepare Tempering
- In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Once hot, add the slit green chilies and curry leaves.
- Saut√© for a minute until they become aromatic.
Step 4: Toss the Fried Paneer
- Add the fried paneer cubes to the pan and toss gently to coat the paneer with the tempering.
- Cook for another 2 minutes so the flavors blend well.
Step 5: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with a side of onion rings or a dipping sauce of your choice.
