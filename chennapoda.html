Chenna Poda

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fresh chenna (cottage cheese)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup semolina (rava)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp cashews, chopped
  • 1 tbsp almonds, chopped
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1 tbsp rose water or kewra water (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp saffron strands (optional)
  • Ghee for greasing

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare Chenna
- If you are using fresh chenna, knead it well to make it smooth.
- If using store-bought chenna, ensure there is no excess water.
- You can use a muslin cloth to drain the excess moisture and press the chenna to make it dry.
Step 2: Mix Ingredients
- In a large bowl, combine the chenna, sugar, semolina (rava), all-purpose flour (maida), milk, ghee, cardamom powder, and salt.
- Add the chopped cashews, almonds, raisins, and saffron strands if using.
- Mix everything thoroughly to form a smooth batter-like mixture.
Step 3: Prepare the Baking Pan
- Grease a round cake tin or a baking dish with ghee.
- You can also line it with parchment paper for easy removal later.
Step 4: Bake the Chenna Poda
- Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
- Pour the prepared mixture into the greased baking tin and level the surface with a spatula.
- Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
Step 5: Cool and Serve
- Once baked, remove from the oven and let it cool completely.
- Once cooled, remove the chenna poda from the tin, slice into pieces, and serve.
Bonus Tips:
- You can drizzle some rose water or kewra water over the chenna poda for a fragrant touch before baking.
- This dessert can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature and is a perfect blend of sweetness and richness.
