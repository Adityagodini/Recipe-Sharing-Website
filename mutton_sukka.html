Mutton Sukka

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (boneless, cut into small cubes)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1/2 cup coconut (fresh or desiccated)
  • 2 tbsp curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • Water as needed

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Mutton
- Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds and fennel seeds, letting them splutter.
- Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cooking for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
Step 2: Cook the Tomatoes
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release oil.
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Stir well to combine.
Step 3: Add Mutton
- Add the mutton pieces to the pan and sauté them until browned on all sides.
- Add enough water to cover the mutton, then bring it to a boil.
- Reduce heat to low and simmer for 30–40 minutes, or until the mutton is tender.
Step 4: Prepare the Coconut Mixture
- In a small pan, dry roast the coconut until it turns light brown and fragrant.
- Let it cool, then grind it into a smooth paste with a little water.
Step 5: Combine and Cook
- Add the coconut paste to the cooked mutton. Stir well and cook for another 10-15 minutes until the gravy thickens.
- If the curry is too dry, add a little water to adjust the consistency.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with rice, roti, or paratha.
Bonus Tip:
- For extra flavor, add a handful of curry leaves while simmering the mutton.
