Jowar Dal

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/2 cup jowar (sorghum) grains
  • 1/2 cup yellow moong dal (split yellow gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh coriander leaves (chopped, for garnish)
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • 1 sprig curry leaves

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Jowar and Moong Dal
- Rinse the jowar grains and moong dal thoroughly.
- In a pressure cooker or pot, add jowar, moong dal, turmeric powder, and water.
- Cook for 4-5 whistles in a pressure cooker or until soft in a pot.
- Mash the dal and jowar slightly with a spoon to combine them into a thicker consistency.
Step 2: Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
- Heat oil or ghee in a pan and add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
- Add hing (asafoetida), dried red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
- Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
- Add chopped tomatoes, slit green chilies, red chili powder, and coriander powder.
- Cook until the tomatoes become soft and release their juices.
Step 3: Combine Dal and Tadka
- Add the cooked jowar and moong dal mixture to the pan with the prepared tadka. Mix well.
- Add water to adjust the consistency of the dal. Bring it to a boil and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
Step 4: Garnish and Serve
- Add garam masala and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
- This dal is best enjoyed with steamed rice or roti.
