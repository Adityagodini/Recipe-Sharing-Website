Dal Khichdi

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/2 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup moong dal (yellow split gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 dried red chili
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1/2 teaspoon green chili (slit, optional)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Step 1: Wash and Soak Rice & Dal
- Rinse the rice and moong dal thoroughly.
- Soak both rice and dal in water for 15-20 minutes (optional but recommended for quicker cooking).
Step 2: Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
- In a pan or pressure cooker, heat ghee or oil and add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and dried red chili.
- Once the seeds splutter, add grated ginger and slit green chilies (optional). Saut√© for 30 seconds.
- Add chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
- Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes soften.
Step 3: Add Rice and Dal
- Add the soaked rice and moong dal to the pan. Stir to combine everything well with the tempering.
Step 4: Cook Dal Khichdi
- Add 2 cups of water (adjust water for desired consistency) and bring it to a boil.
- If cooking in a pressure cooker, cover and cook for 2-3 whistles,
- cook for 10-15 minutes on medium heat until rice and dal are cooked and soft.
- If cooking in a pan, cover and cook for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally,
- until the rice and dal are soft and cooked.
Step 5: Garnish and Serve
- Once cooked, fluff the dal khichdi with a fork to separate the grains.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a drizzle of ghee (optional).
- Serve hot with pickle and yogurt for a complete meal.
