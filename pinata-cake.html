Pinata Cake

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup colorful candies (M&Ms, sprinkles, or any candy of choice)
  • 1 1/2 cups whipped cream
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for whipped cream)
  • Buttercream or fondant for decorating the outside
  • Chocolate chips or more candies for the pinata effect

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Cake Layers
- Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease two round cake pans.
- In a large bowl, sift together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
- In another bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
- Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
- Add the vanilla extract and alternate adding the dry ingredients and buttermilk to the wet mixture.
- Start and finish with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined.
- Divide the batter equally between the two prepared cake pans and smooth the tops.
- Bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
- Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
Step 2: Hollow Out the Center
- Once the cakes are completely cool, take one cake and carefully cut out a circle in the center.
- leaving about 1-inch of the edge intact. This will be your "pinata" shell.
- Reserve the inner portion of the cake and crumble it up into small pieces.
Step 3: Fill the Pinata
- Fill the hollowed-out portion of the cake with colorful candies.
- Place the crumbled cake back inside the hollowed area to seal the candies inside.
Step 4: Prepare the Frosting
- Whip the cream and sugar together until stiff peaks form.
- If you prefer, you can use buttercream or fondant for decorating the cake instead.
Step 5: Assemble the Cake
- Place the filled cake layer on a serving plate. Put the second cake layer on top.
- Cover the entire cake with whipped cream or buttercream to smooth the edges and make the cake look neat.
Step 6: Decorate the Cake
- If using buttercream or fondant, decorate the top and sides of the cake with your preferred design.
- Add extra candy on top for decoration if desired.
Step 7: Cut and Serve
- When you slice the cake, the candies inside will spill out, creating the "pinata" effect!
- Enjoy the surprise and deliciousness of your Pinata Cake!
