Malabar Fish Curry

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fish fillets (Kingfish, Seer Fish, or any firm fish)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 sprigs curry leaves
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (fresh or canned)
  • 1/2 cup tamarind water (or tamarind paste mixed with water)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean the fish fillets thoroughly and cut them into pieces. Set them aside.
Step 2: Heat Oil and Sauté Spices
- Heat coconut oil in a large pan over medium heat.
- Add fennel seeds and curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.
- Add sliced onions and green chilies. Cook until the onions turn soft and golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Ground Spices
- Add chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they soften and release their juices.
- Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Cook the spices for a minute.
Step 4: Add Coconut Milk and Tamarind Water
- Pour in the coconut milk and tamarind water, stirring to combine. Bring the curry base to a simmer.
- Cook the curry for 5-7 minutes until the flavors meld together.
Step 5: Add Fish and Simmer
- Gently add the fish pieces into the curry, making sure they're submerged.
- Let the curry simmer for about 10-12 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.
- Be careful not to stir too much to avoid breaking the fish.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Adjust salt to taste and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
- Serve the Malabar Fish Curry hot with steamed rice, appams, or parottas.
