Bhapa Ilish

INGREDIENTS

  • 2-3 pieces of Hilsa fish (Ilish)
  • 1/2 cup mustard paste (yellow mustard seeds ground into a smooth paste)
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds paste (optional)
  • 1 tbsp yogurt (optional for creaminess)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean the Hilsa fish pieces and remove the skin, if preferred. Rinse them under cold water and pat dry.
- Rub the fish pieces with a little turmeric powder and salt. Let them marinate for about 10 minutes.
Step 2: Make the Mustard Paste
- In a bowl, mix the mustard paste with the poppy seed paste (if using), red chili powder, turmeric powder, and sugar.
- Add water to the paste to form a smooth, thick sauce consistency. Add salt to taste.
Step 3: Cook the Bhapa Ilish
- In a steaming pot or a deep pan, pour the mustard oil and heat it on medium flame.
- Add the mustard paste mixture to the oil and stir well.
- Cook the mixture for 2-3 minutes until the oil begins to separate from the paste.
- Gently place the marinated fish pieces into the pan and cover with the mustard sauce.
- Add the slit green chilies and a little water (about 1/4 cup) to create some steam.
- Cover the pan with a tight lid and cook for 12-15 minutes on low heat.
- If you are using a steamer, place the fish in a steaming tray and steam for about 20-25 minutes.
Step 4: Garnish and Serve
- Once the fish is tender and the sauce has thickened, remove from heat.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.
- Serve the Bhapa Ilish with steamed rice for a traditional Bengali meal.
- The rich mustard sauce enhances the delicate flavor of the fish.
