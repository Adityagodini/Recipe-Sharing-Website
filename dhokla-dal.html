Dhokla Dal

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup moong dal (yellow split gram)
  • 1/2 cup rice
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1/2 teaspoon green chili (slit, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon grated coconut (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Dal
- Wash and soak the moong dal and rice for about 30 minutes to 1 hour.
- Drain the water and blend them into a smooth batter, adding a little water as needed.
- Add turmeric powder and salt to the batter and mix well.
Step 2: Steaming the Dhokla
- Grease a dhokla steaming tray or any round, shallow vessel.
- Pour the batter into the tray, and steam it in a preheated steamer for 15-20 minutes,
- or until a toothpick comes out clean when inserted.
- Once done, let it cool for a few minutes and cut it into small square or diamond-shaped pieces.
Step 3: Prepare the Tempering
- In a small pan, heat the oil. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
- Add grated ginger and slit green chilies (if using). Saut√© for 1-2 minutes.
- Add asafoetida (hing), sugar, and a pinch of salt. Stir well to combine.
- Add 1/4 cup water to the tempering and bring it to a boil.
Step 4: Add Tempering to Dhokla
- Once the tempering is ready, pour it evenly over the steamed dhokla pieces.
Step 5: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, a squeeze of lemon juice, and grated coconut if desired.
- Serve hot with chutney or yogurt on the side.
