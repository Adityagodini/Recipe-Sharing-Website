Kolhapuri Mutton Curry

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in pieces)
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp Kolhapuri masala (or homemade Kolhapuri spice mix)
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste (or fresh tamarind)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • Water as needed

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Mutton
- Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker.
- Add the mutton pieces and brown them on all sides. Remove and set aside.
Step 2: Sauté Onions and Spices
- In the same pot, add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Dry Spices
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and release oil.
- Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and Kolhapuri masala.
- Cook the masalas for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they blend well with the onions and tomatoes.
Step 4: Combine Mutton and Yogurt
- Add the browned mutton back to the pot and mix well with the spice paste.
- Add yogurt and mix until the mutton is coated evenly with the spices and yogurt.
Step 5: Add Tamarind and Simmer
- Add tamarind paste, green chilies, and enough water to make a gravy.
- Mix well and bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 40-45 minutes, or until the mutton becomes tender.
- If using a pressure cooker, cook for 4-5 whistles on medium heat.
Step 6: Final Touches
- Once the mutton is cooked and tender, check for seasoning and adjust with salt or chili powder if necessary.
Step 7: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with naan, chapati, or steamed rice.
Bonus Tip:
- Kolhapuri Mutton Curry is best enjoyed with some extra spice, so adjust the level of Kolhapuri masala to your liking!
