Gonguru Papu

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1.5 to 2 cups gongura (red sorrel) leaves, cleaned and chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2–3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tsp oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2–3 dried red chilies
  • 3–4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 pinch of hing (asafoetida)
  • Curry leaves – few
  • 1/4 tsp mustard seeds – optional

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Dal
- Rinse toor dal and pressure cook with turmeric and 2.5 cups water until soft (3–4 whistles).
Step 2: Cook Gongura
- In a separate pan, cook chopped gongura leaves with green chilies and onion. No need to add water.
- Cook until leaves soften and release their tang.
Step 3: Combine
- Add cooked dal to the softened gongura. Mix well, mash slightly, and simmer for 5–7 minutes. Add salt as needed.
Step 4: Tempering
- Heat oil/ghee in a small pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, red chilies, garlic, hing, and curry leaves. Let it sizzle.
Step 5: Final Touch
- Pour tempering over the dal. Mix gently and let it rest covered for 2 minutes for flavors to infuse.
Step 6: Serve Hot
- Enjoy with hot steamed rice and a dollop of ghee or a crispy vadiyalu. Andhra pride on your plate!
