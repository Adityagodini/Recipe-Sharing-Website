Kolkata Mutton Biryani

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • 1 1/2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp rose water
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp saffron strands soaked in warm milk
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

Recipe

Step 1: Marinate the Mutton
- In a bowl, combine mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, cumin powder, coriander powder.
- turmeric powder, garam masala powder, salt, and lemon juice.
- Let it marinate for at least 2-3 hours, or overnight for better flavor.
Step 2: Prepare the Rice
- In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil with bay leaves, cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom pods, and a pinch of salt.
- Add the soaked rice and cook until 70% done (the rice should still have a bite to it). Drain and set aside.
Step 3: Prepare the Mutton
- Heat oil and ghee in a large pot or pressure cooker. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
- Add marinated mutton to the pot and cook on medium heat until browned and fragrant.
- Add chopped tomatoes, cook until soft, and then lower the heat.
Step 4: Layer the Biryani
- In a large pot, layer the cooked mutton mixture at the bottom.
- Then, layer the partially cooked rice over the mutton.
- Place halved boiled potatoes on top of the rice.
- Sprinkle chopped mint leaves, coriander leaves, and fried onions over the rice.
- Add rose water and kewra water for an aromatic touch.
- Drizzle a little ghee on top and cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid.
Step 5: Cook the Biryani
- Place the pot on low flame for 25-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend .
Step 6: Serve
- Serve the Kolkata Mutton Biryani hot with raita, salad, or a boiled egg on the side.
Bonus Tip:
- For a truly authentic Kolkata Biryani, don’t forget the boiled potatoes, which add a unique flavor and texture to the dish.
