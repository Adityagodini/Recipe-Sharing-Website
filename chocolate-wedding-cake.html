Chocolate Wedding Cake

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 ¾ cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup almond meal
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • ¼ tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Food coloring (optional, for different colors)
  • Buttercream frosting (for filling, any flavor like vanilla, chocolate, or fruit-based)
  • Gold leaf or edible flowers (for decoration, optional)
  • Macaron shells (prepared beforehand)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Macaron Shells
- Preheat your oven to 150°C (300°F) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
- In a food processor, combine powdered sugar and almond meal, then sift the mixture to remove lumps.
- This ensures smooth macaron shells.
- In a clean, dry bowl, beat the egg whites and cream of tartar using an electric mixer until soft peaks form.
- Gradually add granulated sugar and beat until stiff peaks form.
- Gently fold in the almond-sugar mixture into the whipped egg whites, adding food coloring if desired, until fully incorporated.
- The batter should flow smoothly like lava.
- Pipe the batter into small, uniform circles onto the prepared baking sheets using a piping bag fitted with a round tip.
- Tap the baking sheets on the counter to remove air bubbles.
- then let the macarons rest for 30-60 minutes until a skin forms on top of the macarons.
- Bake for 15-20 minutes, then allow the macarons to cool completely on the sheets.
Step 2: Prepare the Buttercream
- While the macarons cool, prepare the buttercream filling.
- Beat 1 cup of softened butter with 4 cups of powdered sugar until smooth. Add flavorings and adjust with milk for consistency.
Step 3: Assemble the Macaron Cake
- Select your largest macaron shells as the base for each tier. Start with a layer of macarons on the bottom tier.
- Use a small amount of buttercream to “glue” the macarons onto a cake board or.
- stand in a circular pattern, stacking them to create a small, stable tower.
- Repeat the process for the remaining tiers, progressively reducing the number of macarons for each tier.
- You can use dowels or supports between the layers to ensure stability if needed.
Step 4: Decorate the Macaron Wedding Cake
- Once the layers are stacked, pipe small swirls of buttercream between each macaron tier to help hold them in place.
- Add additional decoration with edible flowers, gold leaf, or other elegant toppers as desired.
Step 5: Serve the Macaron Wedding Cake
- Your macaron wedding cake is ready to impress your guests! Serve each tier as individual portions for guests to enjoy.
