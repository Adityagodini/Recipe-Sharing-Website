Kasundi Machh

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fish (Rohu, Hilsa, or any firm fish of your choice)
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander powder
  • 2 tbsp Kasundi (Bengali mustard sauce)
  • 1 tbsp mustard seeds (for tempering)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

Step 1: Marinate the Fish
- Clean the fish and make sure it is cut into pieces.
- Marinate the fish pieces with turmeric powder and salt for about 10-15 minutes.
Step 2: Prepare the Base
- Heat mustard oil in a pan or wok over medium heat.
- Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
- Add curry leaves and green chilies, sautéing for a few seconds.
- Add chopped onions and sauté until they become soft and golden brown.
Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Spices
- Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and pulpy.
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Stir well to combine and cook for a couple of minutes.
Step 4: Add Kasundi
- Add the Kasundi (mustard sauce) to the spice mixture and stir well.
- If needed, add a little water to achieve a smooth consistency for the curry.
Step 5: Cook the Fish
- Gently add the marinated fish pieces to the curry and cover it with a lid.
- Cook on medium heat for 10-12 minutes, ensuring the fish is cooked through and absorbs the flavors of the sauce.
- Optionally, add a pinch of sugar to balance the flavors.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Once the fish is cooked, remove from heat.
- Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with steamed rice, paratha, or roti for a traditional Bengali meal.
