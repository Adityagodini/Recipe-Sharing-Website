Gujrati Pigeon Pea Curry

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup pigeon peas (toor dal)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp jaggery (optional for sweetness)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste (for tanginess)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water as required

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Pigeon Peas
- Wash the pigeon peas (toor dal) thoroughly.
- Pressure cook them with 3 cups of water for about 3-4 whistles or until soft. Set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Tempering
- Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
Step 3: SautÃ© the Aromatics
- Add chopped onions, green chili, garlic, and ginger. SautÃ© until onions turn golden brown.
Step 4: Add Tomatoes and Spices
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
- Cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
Step 5: Add Tamarind and Jaggery
- Add tamarind paste and jaggery (if using). Stir well to combine and let the curry simmer for 5-7 minutes.
Step 6: Combine Dal and Curry
- Add the cooked pigeon peas to the pan with some of the cooking water.
- Stir everything well and bring it to a boil. Adjust the consistency of the curry with water as needed.
Step 7: Final Touches
- Add garam masala and mix well. Let the curry simmer for another 5 minutes.
Step 8: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
