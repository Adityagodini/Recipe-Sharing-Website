For Khara Bath:

For Kesari Bath (Sweet):

  • 1 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron / food color (orange/yellow)
  • 2.5 cups water
  • Cashews & raisins (fried in ghee)

Step 1: Roast the Rava
-In a dry pan, roast 1 cup of rava till aromatic and lightly golden. Divide for both dishes.
Step 2: Make Khara Bath
- In a pan, heat oil + ghee, splutter mustard, add dals, green chilies, curry leaves, and onions.
- Saute, then add tomatoes and chopped vegetables. Cook till soft.
- Add turmeric and salt. Pour 2.5 cups water. Bring to a boil.
- Slowly add ¬Ω of the roasted rava while stirring to avoid lumps.
- Cook covered till water is absorbed. Garnish with coriander & lemon juice.
Step 3: Make Kesari Bath
- Boil 2.5 cups water with saffron and sugar.
- Add remaining roasted rava slowly while stirring.
- Add ghee gradually, cardamom powder, and fried cashews & raisins.
- Mix and cook till smooth, ghee separates, and halwa texture forms.
Step 4: Serve the Combo
-On a plate, serve a scoop of Khara Bath and a scoop of Kesari Bath side-by-side.
