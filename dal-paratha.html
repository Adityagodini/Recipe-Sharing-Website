Dal Paratha

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup cooked moong dal (yellow split gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed to knead the dough)
  • Ghee or oil (for cooking)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Dal Filling
- In a bowl, combine the cooked moong dal with cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder.
- garam masala powder, chopped cilantro, and green chili.
- Mash the dal slightly to make a thick paste-like mixture. Add salt to taste and set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Dough
- In a large mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt.
- Slowly add water and knead into a soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
Step 3: Assemble the Parathas
- Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a lemon).
- Roll each dough ball into a small circle. Place a spoonful of the dal mixture in the center of the circle.
- Carefully fold the edges of the dough to encase the dal mixture inside. Pinch the dough together to seal it.
- Roll the stuffed dough ball into a flat disc, making sure the filling doesn't spill out.
Step 4: Cook the Parathas
- Heat a tawa or skillet on medium heat and lightly grease it with ghee or oil.
- Place the rolled-out paratha on the hot tawa and cook for 2-3 minutes on one side, until small bubbles appear.
- Flip and cook the other side, pressing gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking.
- Apply ghee or oil on both sides and cook until golden brown and crispy.
Step 5: Serve
- Serve hot Dal Parathas with yogurt, pickles, or a side of salad.
- Enjoy the warm and flavorful dal-filled parathas with your favorite accompaniment!
