Bhujiya

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 2 tbsp rice flour
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 1/2 tsp black sesame seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp hot oil (for dough)
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Dough
- In a large mixing bowl, combine besan, rice flour, cumin seeds
- carom seeds, sesame seeds, turmeric,powder, red chili powder, and salt.
- Add the hot oil to the flour mixture and mix well. The oil helps to make the bhujia crispy.
- Gradually add water and knead the dough until it’s smooth and soft, but not too sticky.
Step 2: Shape the Bhujia
- Heat oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium heat.
- Fit a sev maker (or a similar tool) with a fine-holed disc and fill it with the dough.
- Squeeze out small portions of dough into the hot oil, creating long, thin strands (sevs).
- Fry the bhujia in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until they are golden brown and crispy.
Step 3: Drain and Cool
- Once the bhujia is crispy and golden, remove it from the oil and drain it on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
- Let them cool completely for a crunchy texture.
Step 4: Serve
- Once the bhujia has cooled, store them in an airtight container.
- Serve with chutney or as a snack with tea. You can also add bhujia as a topping for chaat or bhel puri.
Bonus Tips:
- You can experiment by adding flavors like garlic powder, chili flakes, or dried herbs to the dough for a unique twist.
- If you don’t have a sev maker, you can form small dough balls and press them flat or roll them out thin before frying.
