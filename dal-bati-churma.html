Dal Bati Churma

INGREDIENTS

  • For Dal:
  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • For Bati:
  • 2 cups wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup semolina (rava)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup ghee or melted butter
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (as needed to knead the dough)
  • For Churma:
  • 1 cup wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1/4 cup jaggery or sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Dal
- Wash the toor dal thoroughly. In a pressure cooker, add dal, 4 cups of water, and turmeric powder.
- Cook for 3-4 whistles or until soft.
- In a separate pan, heat ghee and add cumin seeds.
- Once they splutter, add chopped onions, garlic-ginger paste, and green chilies. Cook until golden brown.
- Add chopped tomatoes, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes soften.
- Add the cooked dal to this masala and mix well. Simmer for 5 minutes.
- Add garam masala and adjust consistency with water if needed.
- Garnish with coriander leaves and set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Bati
- In a bowl, mix wheat flour, semolina, baking soda, and salt.
- Add melted ghee and knead into a dough using water. The dough should be stiff but soft.
- Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into round balls (bati).
- Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) and bake the batis for 15-20 minutes, flipping once,
- until they are golden brown. Alternatively, you can bake them on a tandoor or cook on an open flame.
- Once baked, dip the batis in warm ghee for a rich flavor.
Step 3: Prepare the Churma
- In a pan, heat ghee and roast the wheat flour on medium-low heat for about 5-7 minutes, stirring constantly.
- Add jaggery (or sugar) and cardamom powder, and cook until the jaggery melts and combines with the flour.
- Once the mixture is well combined, remove from heat and set aside to cool.
- Crumble the churma mixture into small bits.
Step 4: Serve
- To serve, place the hot dal in a bowl, top with ghee-soaked bati, and serve with churma on the side.
- Enjoy this traditional Rajasthani dish!
