Tamarind Bean Curry

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup dried beans (such as kidney beans, black beans, or chickpeas), soaked overnight
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Beans
- Soak the dried beans overnight.
- Drain and cook them in a pressure cooker with enough water for about 3-4 whistles, or until soft. Set aside.
Step 2: Temper the Spices
- Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter, then add asafoetida (hing).
Step 3: Saut√© the Aromatics
- Add the chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, and minced garlic. Saut√© until the onions become soft and golden brown.
Step 4: Add Tomatoes and Spices
- Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
- Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Let the mixture cook for 3-4 minutes.
Step 5: Add Tamarind Paste
- Add the tamarind paste to the pan and stir well. Let the tangy flavors mix with the spices for 2-3 minutes.
Step 6: Add the Cooked Beans
- Add the cooked beans to the pan and mix well. Add water to adjust the consistency to your liking.
Step 7: Simmer and Cook
- Let the curry simmer for 10-12 minutes so that the beans absorb all the flavors.
Step 8: Final Touches
- Add salt to taste and adjust the spices as needed. Let it cook for another 5 minutes.
Step 9: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
