Methi Thepla Gujarat

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/2 cup fresh fenugreek leaves (methi), finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera)
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for kneading)
  • Water (as needed, for kneading the dough)
  • Ghee or oil (for cooking)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Dough
- In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, chopped methi leaves, turmeric powder,
- red chili powder, cumin seeds, carom seeds, ginger paste, garam masala, and salt.
- Add oil and mix well.
- Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough.
- Cover the dough and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.
Step 2: Roll the Theplas
- Divide the dough into equal-sized balls (about 6-8 balls, depending on size).
- Roll each dough ball into a thin, round disc using a rolling pin, sprinkling some flour
- if needed to avoid sticking.
Step 3: Cook the Theplas
- Heat a tava or flat pan over medium heat.
- Once hot, place the rolled-out thepla on the pan.
- Cook for about 1-2 minutes until you see bubbles forming on the surface.
- Flip the thepla and cook for another 1 minute, then drizzle some ghee or oil on top and flip again.
- Press gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking and golden brown spots on both sides.
- Repeat with the remaining dough balls.
Step 4: Serve
- Serve the hot methi thepla with yogurt, pickle, or any curry of your choice.
