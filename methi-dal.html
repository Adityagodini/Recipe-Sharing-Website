Methi Dal

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup yellow moong dal (split yellow lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tomato (pureed)
  • 2-3 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1-inch ginger (grated)
  • 2 cloves garlic (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 cup fresh methi (fenugreek) leaves (washed and chopped)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Lentils
- Rinse the moong dal and soak it in water for 15-20 minutes.
- In a pressure cooker, add the soaked moong dal, turmeric powder, and water.
- Cook for 3-4 whistles or until the dal becomes soft and mushy.
- Mash the dal slightly to get a smooth consistency.
Step 2: Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
- Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
- Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
- Add chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
- Add the pureed tomato and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
Step 3: Add Spices
- Add coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, cumin powder, and salt.
- Stir the mixture and cook for another minute.
Step 4: Add Methi Leaves
- Add the chopped methi (fenugreek) leaves and sauté for 2-3 minutes until the leaves are wilted and cooked down.
Step 5: Combine Dal and Tadka
- Add the cooked and mashed moong dal to the pan. Stir well to combine.
- Add water if needed to adjust the consistency of the dal.
- Simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together.
Step 6: Final Touch
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice (optional).
Step 7: Serve
- Serve hot with steamed rice or roti. Enjoy the wholesome and nutritious Methi Dal!
