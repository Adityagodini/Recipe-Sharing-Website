Kerala Fish Curry

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g fish (preferably kingfish, tilapia, or any firm fish)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 2-3 sprigs curry leaves
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1-2 tsp tamarind paste (or as per taste)
  • 1 cup coconut milk (fresh or canned)
  • 1 cup water (adjust as needed for consistency)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean the fish and cut into medium-sized pieces. Marinate the fish with a pinch of turmeric powder and salt for 10-15 minutes.
Step 2: Prepare the Curry Base
- Heat coconut oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
- Add mustard seeds and let them splutter, then add curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds.
- Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown and soft.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
Step 3: Add Tomatoes and Spices
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Stir well and cook for another 2 minutes.
Step 4: Add Coconut Milk and Tamarind
- Add the coconut milk and tamarind paste to the pan, followed by water. Mix well and bring it to a boil.
- Reduce the heat and simmer for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the curry to thicken slightly.
Step 5: Cook the Fish
- Gently add the marinated fish pieces into the curry and stir carefully. Make sure the fish is submerged in the gravy.
- Cook on medium heat for 10-15 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and tender.
- Add salt to taste, and adjust the consistency by adding more water if necessary.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Once the fish is cooked and the curry has thickened, remove the pan from the heat.
- Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
- Serve hot with steamed rice, appam, or parotta for a complete Kerala meal.
