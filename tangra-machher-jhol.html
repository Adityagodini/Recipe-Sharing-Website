Tangra Machher Jhol

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g Tangra fish (or Catla, Rohu fish can be used as an alternative)
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1-2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as required to make curry consistency)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean and wash the Tangra fish pieces thoroughly. Pat them dry using a kitchen towel.
- Rub the fish pieces with a little turmeric and salt and let them rest for about 10 minutes.
Step 2: Fry the Fish
- Heat mustard oil in a frying pan. When the oil is hot, carefully fry the fish pieces until they are golden brown on both sides.
- Set aside on a plate.
Step 3: Prepare the Curry Base
- In the same pan, add a bit more mustard oil and heat it. Add cumin seeds, nigella seeds, and slit green chilies. Let them splutter.
- Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they become soft and golden.
- Add ginger and garlic paste and fry for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until the tomatoes become soft and mushy.
- Now, add turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Stir well and cook the spices for a minute.
Step 4: Cook the Potatoes
- Add the cubed potatoes to the pan and mix them well with the spice paste.
- Pour in about 1-1.5 cups of water and bring the mixture to a boil.
- Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the potatoes to cook and absorb the flavors.
Step 5: Add Fish to the Curry
- Gently add the fried fish pieces to the curry. Make sure the fish is submerged in the gravy.
- Add salt to taste and cook on medium heat for about 10-12 minutes, allowing the fish to soak up the flavors of the curry.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Once the curry reaches a nice consistency, garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
- Serve the Tangra Machher Jhol hot with steamed rice. Enjoy the spicy, tangy flavors!
