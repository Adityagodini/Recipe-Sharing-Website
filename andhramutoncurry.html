Andhra Mutton Curry

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in pieces)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds (khus-khus)
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp ground roasted peanuts
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Spice Paste
- Dry roast the poppy seeds, sesame seeds, and dried red chilies for 2-3 minutes in a pan until aromatic.
- Grind the roasted ingredients along with roasted peanuts into a smooth paste, adding a little water if necessary.
Step 2: Cook the Mutton
- Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
- Add the finely chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
- Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
- Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil begins to separate.
Step 3: Add Ground Spices
- Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and garam masala powder to the pan.
- Stir well and cook for 2 minutes.
Step 4: Add Mutton and Simmer
- Add the mutton pieces and stir well to coat them with the spices.
- Add water to the pan, enough to cover the mutton. Bring to a boil.
- Reduce the heat and cover the pot. Let it simmer for 1.5-2 hours or until the mutton becomes tender.
- Stir occasionally and adjust the water level as needed.
Step 5: Add Tamarind and Garnish
- Once the mutton is cooked, add the tamarind paste and salt. Mix well and simmer for another 10 minutes.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Step 6: Serve
- Serve the Andhra Mutton Curry hot with steamed rice, chapati, or biryani.
Bonus Tip:
- You can add a little jaggery for a subtle sweetness to balance the heat and tanginess.
