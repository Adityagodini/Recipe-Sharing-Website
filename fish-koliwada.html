Fish Koliwada

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g boneless fish fillets (Kingfish, Sole, or similar fish)
  • 1/2 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • Oil for deep frying

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean the fish fillets thoroughly and cut them into bite-sized pieces.
- Pat them dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.
Step 2: Marinate the Fish
- In a mixing bowl, combine turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, cumin powder, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, and salt.
- Add the fish pieces to this marinade and coat the fish well. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes for the flavors to infuse.
Step 3: Prepare the Batter
- In another bowl, mix chickpea flour, rice flour, and baking soda (if using).
- Add chopped green chilies, fresh coriander, and a pinch of salt to the flour mixture.
- Gradually add water to make a thick batter that will coat the fish pieces.
Step 4: Deep Fry the Fish
- Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium-high heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
- Dip the marinated fish pieces into the batter, making sure each piece is well coated.
- Gently slide the battered fish pieces into the hot oil and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy.
- Fry in batches to avoid overcrowding.
- Remove the fried fish from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
Step 5: Serve
- Serve the Fish Koliwada hot with lemon wedges and a side of green chutney.
- Garnish with chopped onions and coriander leaves for extra flavor.
- Enjoy this crispy and spicy fish appetizer with a cold drink!
