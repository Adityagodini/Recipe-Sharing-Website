Dal Amritsari

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup yellow dal (split yellow moong dal)
  • 1/2 cup masoor dal (red lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2-3 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1-inch ginger (grated)
  • 2 cloves garlic (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon hing (asafoetida)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Lentils
- Rinse the yellow dal and masoor dal in water and soak them for 15-20 minutes.
- In a pressure cooker, add the soaked lentils with water and turmeric powder.
- Cook for 3-4 whistles or until the dal is soft.
- Mash the dal slightly to get a creamy consistency.
Step 2: Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
- Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
- Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
- Add chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until aromatic.
- Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
Step 3: Add Spices
- Add garam masala, red chili powder, coriander powder, and hing to the mixture.
- Stir well and cook for a minute.
Step 4: Combine Dal and Tadka
- Add the cooked and mashed dal to the pan. Stir well and adjust the consistency with water if needed.
- Let the dal simmer for 5-7 minutes on medium heat, allowing all the flavors to blend together.
Step 5: Final Touch
- Add kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) and salt to taste. Stir and cook for another minute.
Step 6: Garnish
- Garnish the Dal Amritsari with fresh coriander leaves.
Step 7: Serve
- Serve hot with steamed rice or naan. Enjoy the rich and flavorful Dal Amritsari!
