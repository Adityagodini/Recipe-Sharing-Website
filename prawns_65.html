Prawns 65

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g prawns (cleaned and deveined)
  • 2 tbsp rice flour
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2-3 tbsp yogurt
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • Fresh curry leaves (for garnish)
  • Oil for frying

Recipe

Step 1: Marinate the Prawns
- In a bowl, mix prawns with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder.
- garam masala, cumin powder, ajwain, black pepper, salt, lemon juice, and yogurt.
- Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, or if you have time.,
- allow it to sit for an hour for the flavors to penetrate.
Step 2: Prepare the Coating
- In another bowl, mix rice flour, cornflour, and all-purpose flour together.
- You can add more chili powder if you like extra heat.
Step 3: Coat the Prawns
- Coat each marinated prawn with the flour mixture, ensuring it’s evenly coated.
- If you’re using an egg, dip the prawns in the beaten egg before coating with the flour mixture for extra crispiness.
Step 4: Heat Oil
- Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat.
- The oil should be hot but not smoking.
- You can test this by dropping a small amount of flour into the oil; it should sizzle immediately.
Step 5: Fry the Prawns
- Gently drop the prawns into the hot oil in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
- Fry for about 2-3 minutes, or until the prawns turn golden brown and crispy.
Step 6: Drain and Garnish
- Once fried, remove the prawns from the oil and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
- Garnish with fresh curry leaves for that signature Prawns 65 flavor.
Step 7: Serve
- Serve hot with lemon wedges, onion rings, and green chutney as a perfect snack or appetizer!
