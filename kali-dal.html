Kali Dal

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup black urad dal (whole black gram)
  • 1/4 cup toor dal (yellow pigeon peas)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Soak and Cook the Lentils
- Rinse the black urad dal and toor dal thoroughly.
- Soak them for at least 4-5 hours or overnight for quicker cooking.
- In a pressure cooker, add the soaked dals, turmeric powder, and enough water to cover the dals.
- Cook for 4-5 whistles or until the dals are soft and tender.
- If using a regular pot, cook the dals until soft, adding water as needed.
- Mash the dal lightly with a back of a spoon for a thicker consistency, or leave it chunky for a textured dal.
Step 2: Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
- Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add cumin seeds and allow them to splutter.
- Add chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
- Add pureed tomatoes and cook for 5-6 minutes until the oil separates from the masala.
Step 3: Add Spices
- Add coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, cumin powder, and salt.
- Stir well and cook the masala for a few more minutes.
Step 4: Combine Dal and Tadka
- Add the cooked dal to the pan with the prepared tadka and mix well.
- Add water to adjust the consistency of the dal if necessary.
- Add kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) for a smoky flavor.
- and simmer the dal on low heat for 10-15 minutes to allow the spices to infuse.
Step 5: Final Touch
- Stir in fresh cream (optional) for richness. Check the seasoning and adjust salt as needed.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
