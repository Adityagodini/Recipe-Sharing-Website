Panner Pulao

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 large onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1/2 cup peas (frozen or fresh)
  • 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom pods
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Rice
- Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
- Soak the rice in water for 20-30 minutes and drain the water before cooking.
Step 2: Fry the Paneer
- Heat 1 tablespoon of oil or ghee in a pan.
- Fry the cubed paneer pieces until golden brown and crispy on all sides. Remove and set aside.
Step 3: Sauté the Spices and Vegetables
- In the same pan, add the remaining oil or ghee.
- Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom pods.
- Let them splutter for a few seconds.
- Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
- Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 1-2 minutes.
- Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, peas, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala powder.
- Cook until the tomatoes soften and the spices blend well.
Step 4: Cook the Rice
- Add the drained rice to the pan and gently stir to coat the rice with the spices.
- Add 1 1/2 cups of water and salt to taste. Bring it to a boil.
- Reduce the heat, cover the pan with a lid, and cook the rice on low heat for 15-20 minutes,
- or until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed.
Step 5: Add the Paneer
- Once the rice is cooked, gently fluff it with a fork. Add the fried paneer cubes to the rice and mix well.
- Let the pulao sit covered for 5 minutes to let the flavors combine.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with raita, pickle, or any side of your choice.
