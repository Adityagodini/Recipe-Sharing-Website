Bombil Fry

INGREDIENTS

  • 8-10 Bombil (Bombay Duck) fish
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1-2 green chilies (optional, for extra spice)
  • Oil for frying (preferably coconut oil)
  • Water as needed to make a batter

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Fish
- Clean the Bombil fish by removing the head and any internal organs. Gently rinse them under cold water.
- Pat them dry using paper towels. Be careful as Bombil is a delicate fish.
- Optionally, make a few shallow cuts on both sides of each fish to help the batter stick better.
Step 2: Make the Marinade
- In a mixing bowl, combine turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder.
- cumin powder, garam masala, ginger-garlic paste, and salt.
- Add lemon juice to this mixture and make a smooth marinade.
- Coat each fish piece with this marinade and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse.
Step 3: Prepare the Coating
- In a shallow plate, mix rice flour and chickpea flour. This will create the crispy coating for the fish.
- Add some additional salt and red chili powder to the flour mix for extra flavor.
- Lightly dredge each marinated fish in this flour mixture, ensuring the fish is fully coated on both sides.
Step 4: Fry the Fish
- Heat oil in a large frying pan or shallow skillet over medium heat.
- Once the oil is hot, carefully place the fish in the pan, making sure not to overcrowd them.
- Fry the fish for 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown and crispy. Turn them gently to avoid breaking the delicate fish.
Step 5: Garnish and Serve
- Once fried, remove the Bombil fry from the pan and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
- Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve immediately with lime wedges and green chilies for extra zing.
- Serve the crispy Bombil Fry with hot rice or as a delicious starter with chutneys.
