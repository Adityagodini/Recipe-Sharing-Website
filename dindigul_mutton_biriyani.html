Dindigul Mutton Biryani

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • 1 1/2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp rose water (optional)

Recipe

Step 1: Marinate the Mutton
- In a bowl, mix the mutton with yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder.
- garam masala, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, and salt.
- Add lemon juice and rose water (if using) for extra fragrance. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour .
Step 2: Prepare the Rice
- In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add fennel seeds, bay leaf, and a pinch of salt.
- Add the soaked rice and cook until 70% done (the rice should still have a bite to it). Drain and set aside.
Step 3: Prepare the Biryani Base
- Heat oil and ghee in a large pan. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
- Add the marinated mutton to the pan and cook until the mutton is browned on all sides (about 10 minutes).
- Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and the oil starts separating from the masala.
Step 4: Layer the Biryani
- In a large pot, layer the cooked mutton mixture at the bottom.
- Then, layer the partially cooked rice over the mutton.
- Sprinkle chopped coriander, mint leaves, and fried onions over the rice.
- Drizzle some ghee on top and cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid.
Step 5: Cook the Biryani
- Place the pot on a low flame and cook for about 25-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
- Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.
Step 6: Serve
- Serve the Dindigul Mutton Biryani hot with raita or salan (a spicy curry) on the side.
Bonus Tip:
- For an authentic touch, use a pressure cooker for cooking the mutton, but be careful not to overcook it!
