Vangi Bath

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup rice (washed and drained)
  • 1 medium-sized eggplant (vangi), cut into small cubes
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon jaggery (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup peanuts (optional, for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (optional, for added flavor)

Recipe

Step 1: Cook the Rice
- Wash and drain the rice. Cook it in a pot or rice cooker with 1.5 cups of water.
- Let it cook and fluff it up once done. Set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Eggplant
- Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pan. Add the cubed eggplant and sautÃ© it for 5-7 minutes
- until it softens and turns golden brown. Set aside.
Step 3: Tempering
- In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil. Once hot, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and asafoetida.
- When the seeds splutter, add the slit green chilies and sautÃ© for 30 seconds.
Step 4: Spice Mixture
- Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and a pinch of salt. Stir to combine the spices with the oil.
- Add the cooked eggplant cubes to the pan and mix well, letting the spices coat the eggplant.
Step 5: Add Rice
- Add the cooked rice to the pan with the eggplant and spice mixture.
- Gently mix the rice and vegetables together, ensuring the rice is evenly coated with the spices.
Step 6: Add Tamarind and Jaggery
- Add tamarind paste and jaggery (if using) to the rice mixture. Adjust salt if needed and stir well to combine.
Step 7: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish the Vangi Bath with chopped coriander leaves and peanuts (optional).
