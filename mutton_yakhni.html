Mutton Yakhni

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or clarified butter
  • 1 large onion, sliced thinly
  • 1-inch piece ginger, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt, whisked
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Recipe

Step 1: Sauté the Whole Spices
- Heat ghee in a large pot over medium heat. Add cardamom, cloves, cinnamon stick, bay leaf.
- cumin seeds, and black peppercorns. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
Step 2: Brown the Mutton
- Add sliced onions to the pot and cook until they turn golden brown.
- Add the ginger and garlic slices, and sauté until aromatic, about 2-3 minutes.
- Add the mutton pieces and sear them until they are browned on all sides.
Step 3: Cook the Yakhni
- Add whisked yogurt to the pot, stirring well to coat the mutton evenly.
- Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt. Bring the mixture to a boil.
- Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours.
- until the mutton becomes tender and the flavors are absorbed.
Step 4: Strain the Yakhni
- Once the mutton is cooked, strain the broth to separate the solids from the liquid.
- The resulting liquid is the yakhni.
Step 5: Serve
- Serve the yakhni hot in bowls, garnished with fresh coriander leaves.
- You can also serve it with naan, rice, or any bread of your choice.
Bonus Tip:
- For extra richness, you can add a splash of cream just before serving.
