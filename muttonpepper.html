Mutton Pepper Fry

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g mutton (cut into small pieces)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder (freshly ground)
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2 cloves
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Recipe

Step 1: Prepare the Mutton
- Wash the mutton pieces thoroughly and set them aside.
- You can marinate the mutton with a pinch of turmeric and salt for 30 minutes for better flavor (optional).
Step 2: Cook the Spices
- Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, fennel seeds, dried red chilies, cinnamon stick, and cloves.
- Fry for a minute until they release their aroma.
- Add the sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
- Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Fry for another minute.
Step 3: Add Ground Spices
- Add the black pepper powder, coriander powder, and turmeric powder.
- Stir to combine and cook for 2-3 minutes until the spices are well-blended.
Step 4: Add Mutton and Simmer
- Add the mutton pieces to the pan and stir well to coat them with the spices.
- Cook the mutton on medium-high heat for 5-7 minutes until it starts to brown on the edges.
- Add a little water (about 1/2 cup) and bring the mixture to a boil.
- Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes or until the mutton is tender.
- Stir occasionally and add more water if needed.
Step 5: Finish the Dish
- Once the mutton is cooked, add garam masala powder and mix well.
- If using coconut milk, add it now to make the dish richer.
- Simmer for another 5 minutes until everything is well-combined.
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
- Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
- Serve the Mutton Pepper Fry hot with steamed rice, roti, or paratha.
Bonus Tip:
- Adjust the amount of black pepper based on your spice tolerance.
- For a more intense flavor, add freshly ground black pepper just before serving.
